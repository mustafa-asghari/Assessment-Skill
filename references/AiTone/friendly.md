# Friendly tone

Use a warm, clear tone that still respects the assessment and marker.

- Explain choices in plain language.
- Use first person where reflection, decisions, or workplace actions are required.
- Keep the writing natural, but avoid casual filler.
- Use specific examples instead of broad positive claims.
- Let the work sound like a capable student explaining what they did and why.
- Keep headings and structure aligned with the rubric or template.

Default style target: approachable, practical, and credible.
