# Formal tone

Use a precise, professional tone suitable for assessors, workplace supervisors, and formal academic submissions.

- Prefer clear topic sentences and direct evidence.
- Use first person only when the task asks for reflection, workplace experience, or personal action.
- Keep claims measured and supported.
- Avoid slang, jokes, excessive emotion, sales language, and dramatic phrasing.
- Use field terminology when it is accurate and expected by the rubric.
- Vary sentence length, but keep paragraphs controlled and easy to mark.
- Do not over-polish the work into generic corporate language.

Default style target: calm, specific, evidence-led, and assessor-friendly.
