# Informal tone

Use a simple, direct tone only when the assessment allows informal writing, reflective journaling, peer discussion, or workplace notes.

- Use everyday wording without sounding careless.
- Keep technical terms where the rubric needs them.
- Use first person naturally when describing actions, choices, mistakes, or learning.
- Avoid slang that could look unprofessional.
- Do not force jokes, dramatic opinions, or exaggerated personality.
- Keep the answer easy to mark and tied to the task.

Default style target: plain, honest, and human, while still meeting the assessment rules.
